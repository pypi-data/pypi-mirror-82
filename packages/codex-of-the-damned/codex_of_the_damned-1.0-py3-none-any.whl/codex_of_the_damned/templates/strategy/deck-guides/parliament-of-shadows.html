Summary

This is a Lasombra Stealth, Bleed & Vote deck. The setup is to get a the oldest vampire out first, then play {{ card("Govern the Unaligned") }} at superior D to get more Lasombra in play. Each vampire can undertake strong bleed and strong votes, and the high level of stealth make them hard to block.

Offence

Pool damage is very high, using {{ card("Kine Resources Contested") }} and {{ card("Govern the Unaligned") }}, modified by {{ card("Conditioning") }}.

This strong offence is supported by a strong stealth module of 20 action modifiers. {{ card("Shadow Play") }}, {{ card("Shroud of Absence") }} and {{ card("Shroud of Night") }} improve stealth, {{ card("Tenebrous Form") }} can even be used at superior £ to deny block from an ally (as they have no blood to spend). Finally, {{ card("Blanket of Night") }} and {{ card("Seduction") }} are excellent block denials in case the prey stacks too much intercept to get the actions through.

Finally, along damage dealing votes, {{ card("Banishment") }} makes for an excellent vampire remover since the capacity of the deck vampires is high. Played on a vampire with low blood, it forces the Methusalah to pay pool again to get her back into play.

Defence

The bloat module is strong, using {{ card("Villein") }} to get blood back from the big capacity vampires, as well as two strong bloating votes: {{ card("Ancient Influence") }} and {{ card("Political Stranglehold") }}.

Intercept is low, with {{ card("Under Siege") }} being the only intercept tool (although a very good one).

The combat module is strictly defensive, with {{ card("Oubliette") }} and {{ card("Shadow Body") }} for "Strike: Combat Ends“ and {{ card("Obedience") }} to avoid the combat altogether.

The deck should be carfeul about what to intercept as it needs to avoid difficult combats. Wakes are available with {{ card("On the Qui Vive") }} and {{ card("Wake with Evening’s Freshness") }}, to be able to play {{ card("Deflection") }} while locked.

Tips

Beware that some decks include {{ card("Archon Investigation") }}: bleeding over 3 is a risk for the vampire, only the most disposable should be risked this way.

Early game, use {{ card("Govern the Unaligned") }} at superior D two to four times: stay modest and hidden during this build-up and avoid putting too much pressure on your prey too soon.

Avoid combat, try to block only if you have an {{ card("Obedience") }} ready.

Do not waste stealth if you are not confident you can get the action through. Better to use a "Strike: Combat Ends" or hope the combat does not go too bad, than to play an insufficeint stealth modifier and hope to draw another one.

Lunge

The deck has great forward power, but using it well is tricky. Too much pressure too soon and the prey will keep most of its pool for defence, you will advantage your grand-prey too much and attract the table attention. Too little, and you will not go as fast as you could.

The usual strategy to optimize this pressure is to try to build a lunge hand. The idea is to gather a good aggressive combination in hand to oust your prey in a single turn.

For example, if you have three vampires out, try to keep one or two {{ card("Kine Resources Contested") }}, a {{ card("Govern the Unaligned") }}, a {{ card("Conditioning") }} and some stealth, for a maximum total damage of 12 pool. Until you get there, keep doing 3-5 damage per turn and deflecting your predator bleed, slowly grinding your prey's pool and letting her believe 12 pool is a comfortable cushion.
